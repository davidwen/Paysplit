What is Paysplit?

Paysplit is a site designed to help you and your peers manage expenses together. Paysplit makes it easier for people to share expenses with others and keep track of who owes who what.

What isn't Paysplit?

Paysplit does not do any actual transactions for you. It is only a reporting service that makes it easier to track how much you owe and how much is owed to you. If you report a payment to someone on Paysplit, don't forgot to actually make the payment!

How does Paysplit work?

Users can submit three types of transactions: Expenses, Dues, and Payments.

  • An Expense is a transaction that users make that they want to share with others. For example, if a user took the bill for a $40 group dinner and wants to share the expense with the other diners, that user would want to submit a $40 Expense.

  • A Due is a transaction that represents an amount that one user owes to another. Dues are created from Expenses. When a user creates an Expense, he also creates Dues for each person that owes money as a result of the Expense. For example, if the dinner payer from earlier wants to share the $40 dinner with three friends, he would create three Dues of $10 from his friends to him.

  • A Payment is a transaction from one user to another user. Payments will usually be submitted to pay off debts. For example, after the $10 Due was created earlier, the borrower may submit a $10 Payment to the dinner payer after the borrower's share of the dinner is paid back.

Expenses and Dues are submitted together in the Submit Expense link in the top bar. Payments are submitted in the Submit Payment link in the top bar. To see a history of transactions that you were involved in, go to the Transactions link in the top bar.

How do I check how much I owe to others?

Paysplit keeps track of Balances between each user. A Balance simply represents how much someone owes to another. As transactions are submitted, Paysplit updates Balances so users can keep track of how much they owe to others and how much others owe to them. Balances can be checked in the Balances link in the top bar.
